Physics chapters such as Rotation depend strongly on Vectors, Kinematics, Newton's Laws of Motion, and Work Power Energy.

Organic Chemistry improves when students connect chemical bonding, electronic effects, mechanisms, and repeated reaction pattern recall.

Integration requires confidence in differentiation, trigonometry, algebraic manipulation, and application of derivatives.

For Rotation, revise torque, angular momentum, rolling motion, and energy links with Work Power Energy before attempting advanced mixed problems.

For Kinematics weakness, repair vectors, relative motion, graph interpretation, and equation selection before moving to NLM and Rotation.

For Calculus readiness, alternate formula recall with application questions. Integration improves when substitution, parts, and definite integral properties are practiced separately before mixed quizzes.
